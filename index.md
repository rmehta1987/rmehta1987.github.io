I am a PhD student in the Bioengineering Department at University of Illinois at Chicago. My main interests lie in statistical machine learning. Specifically, the confluence of Bayesian inference and deep learning for the joint modeling of genomics and medical imaging in cancerous patients, whereby we can predict a patient's genotype given the corresponding medical image.

I also try to understand the underlying structure of 'omics datasets, in particular somatic mutations. I use Bayesian non-parametrics to create an unsupervised model for determining positive and negative interactions of somatic mutation in cancer. Biologically relevant interactions will allow us to identify patient cancer subtypes and latent confounders that drive the complexity of interactions somatic mutations. The ultimate goal is to identify co-occurring somatic mutations that are of interest in targeted treatment.

Through the couse of my PhD I took various notes of my research on this website and on my personal laptop. You'll find some updated reflections of my old notes as well recent posts on this blog.
