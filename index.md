I am currently a Postdoctral Scholar at the University of Chicago Human Genetics Department with [Dr. Jeremy Berg](http://www.jjbpopgen.org/).  My Postdoc  
interests lie in learning evolutionary theory using statistical machine learning and first principals.  

I was a PhD student in the Bioengineering Department at University of Illinois at Chicago advised by [Dr. Muge Karaman](https://mkaraman.people.uic.edu/). My PhD interests lie in statistical machine learning. Specifically, the confluence of Bayesian inference and deep learning for the joint modeling of genomics and medical imaging in cancerous patients, whereby we can predict a patient's genotype given the corresponding medical image.

I also try to understand the underlying structure of 'omics datasets, in particular the interactions between somatic mutations in human cancers. This is done using Bayesian non-parametrics to create an unsupervised model for determining latent confounders that influence positive and negative interactions between somatic mutation. Biologically relevant interactions will allow us to identify cancer subtypes and determine the co-occurring somatic mutations that drive cancer. The ultimate goal is to identify sets of somatic mutations that are of interest in targeted treatment.
