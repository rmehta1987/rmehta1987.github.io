In my undergrad I worked on [object detection](http://vision.stanford.edu/srvc/ "Vision"), where my then mentors were also working on [ImageNet](http://www.image-net.org/ "ImageNet") database.  Upon entering the PhD program, I stayed with this theme by focusing on finding cancerous lesions and segmentation of Lung Cancer.  I found papers using Bayesian modeling for image segmentation, but I believed probabilistic techniques seemed a little too complicated for this task, (but I wanted to learn more about Bayesian modeling).  During this time was also rise of deep learning architectures, [AlexNet](https://papers.nips.cc/paper/4824-imagenet-classification-with-deep-convolutional-neural-networks.pdf) in 2012, and it seemed to forgo traditional hand-crafted features for more data-driven features via Convolutional Neural Networks.  I kind of lost research in this area, and shifted towards the question of how else can we use information in medical imaging ? Instead classifying lesions as benign/malignant, can we use them to generate/predict treatment response or data from other 'omics.
